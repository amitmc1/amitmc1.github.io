About Me

I am a PhD researcher in computational chemistry at Cardiff University, where I integrate advanced AI algorithms with quantum mechanical modelling to develop cost-effective and transferable workflows for materials modelling. I am part of the interdisciplinary Prosperity Partnership Sustainable Catalysis for Clean Growth, working with partners in industry (Johnson Matthey and bp, through the bp-ICAM) and academia (University of Manchester) to advance the fundamental understanding of complex catalytic materials for clean and sustainable energy.

Software & Libraries

  • Quantum Chemistry: VASP, FHI-aims, PySCF
  • Materials Modelling: ASE, Pymatgen, DScribe, PyTorch-Geometric
  • Bayesian Optimisation and Monte Carlo: GPyOpt, SuSMoST
  • Machine Learned Interatomic Potentials: MACE
  • ML Algorithms: SISSO++, gplearn, PySR, PyTorch, Scikit-learn
  • Large Language Models: GPT-4o, Mathstral-7B

Research Interests and Experience

  • Generative AI for inverse catalyst design, including variational autoencoders and latent diffusion models
  • Integrated workflows for high-dimensional optimisation in materials modelling, incorporating symbolic regression, support vector machines, Bayesian optimisation, deep neural networks and large language models
  • Electronic structure modelling of complex oxides, including DFT+U and hybrid-DFT
  • Accelerating materials modelling using machine learned DFT functionals and interatomic potentials
  • High performance computing on CPU and GPU nodes; validating theoretical predictions and extracting useful information from experimental characterisation (EPR, XANES, TEM, XPS)
