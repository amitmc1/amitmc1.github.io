--- layout: archive title: "Physics-Informed Machine Learning for Modelling Defect-Driven Catalytic Phenomena" permalink: /phdthesis/ author_profile: true --- {% include base_path %} {% for post in site.phdthesis %} {% include archive-single.html %} {% endfor %} I completed my PhD under the supervision of Dr Andrew Logsdail at the Cardiff Catalysis Institute, in collaboration with Johnson Matthey and the bp International Centre for Advanced Materials (bp-ICAM). My research combines quantum mechanical simulations with modern machine learning approaches to understand how defects influence the performance of catalytic materials:
* Developing new methods for determining DFT+U parameters that enable accurate and efficient simulations of polarons in transition metal and rare-earth oxides. * Applying DFT-parameterised statistical algorithms and interatomic potentials to investigate design strategies for sulfur-tolerant industrial catalysts.
