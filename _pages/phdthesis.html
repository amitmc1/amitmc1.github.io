--- layout: archive title: "My PhD Research in Computational Chemistry" permalink: /phdthesis/ author_profile: true --- {% include base_path %} {% for post in site.phdthesis %} {% include archive-single.html %} {% endfor %} I completed my PhD under the supervision of Dr Andrew Logsdail at the Cardiff Catalysis Institute, in collaboration with Johnson Matthey and the bp International Centre for Advanced Materials (bp-ICAM), as part of the EPSRC-funded Prosperity Partnership Sustainable Catalysis for Clean Growth. My thesis is titled "Physics-Informed Machine Learning for Modelling Defect-Driven Catalytic Phenomena", focusing on the development and application of machine learning-aided quantum mechanical simulations to understand how defects influence the performance of catalytic materials.
  • Developing new machine-learning methods for determining DFT+U parameters that enable accurate and efficient simulations of polarons in transition metal and rare-earth oxides.
  • Applying DFT-parameterised statistical algorithms and interatomic potentials to investigate design strategies for sulfur-tolerant industrial catalysts.
